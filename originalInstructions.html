This is a visual search task where you will be required to correctly pick a target out of an array of distractors.

On each trial, you will see many bars that look like "L" shapes, these are the distractors.

Your job is to find the target "T" shape hidden among the "L"s.

Here are examples of both "L" and "T" shapes.

"L" Shapes (distractors)
"T" Shapes (targets)

The rotation of the "L" and "T" shapes can vary, so you may need to look very closely to find the targets.

Here are some example arrays. Each array has a maximum of one "T" or no "T"s at all.

For example:

"T" - Present Trial
"T" - Absent Trial

There will never be more than one T in each array.

If you find a "T", use your mouse to select it with a click. An empty circle to mark your selection will appear if you click on either a "T" or a "L". You can only make ONE selection per trial.

If the array does NOT contain a "T", do NOT click on anything.

When you have finished searching, press the SPACEBAR to indicate that you are ready to move on.

Excellent searchers are not only accurate, but also fast and precise. Please do your best to:

  1. Correctly find the target "T"
  2. Avoid clicking on any distractor "L"s
  3. Finish searching the array as quickly as possible

A black circle will appear at the center of the display after you press the SPACEBAR.

Hover your cursor over the circle to proceed to the next trial:

In practice trials ONLY: After pressing the SPACEBAR, your selection will fill with a colored shape which reflects whether it was correct or incorrect.

A green circle indicates a correct target selection, and a red triangle indicates an incorrect selection. A missed target will be circled in red.

For example:

Incorrect

This feedback is only available during practice. In the main experiment, you WILL NOT see any of these markers after pressing the SPACEBAR.

On each trial, you have XXX seconds to search the array and press SPACEBAR to move on. If you take longer than this, the trial is considered failed.

That is all of the instructions! Now, you have a chance to practice the task before beginning the main experiment.
